Scatter plot showing predicted height (x-axis, in cm) vs actual height (y-axis, in cm) for 2,000 individuals.

Points are colored by sex: blue circles = Males, red circles = Females. The two clouds are clearly separated, with males clustered at higher heights (~165-195 cm) and females at lower heights (~148-178 cm). Both groups show a positive linear relationship between predicted and actual height.

Vertical error bars are shown at intervals along the fitted regression lines for each sex group, indicating variance in actual height within predicted height bins.

The plot demonstrates that polygenic risk score-based height prediction is reasonably accurate and sex-stratified.
