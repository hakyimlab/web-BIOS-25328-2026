Multi-panel figure showing differences in allele frequency and linkage disequilibrium (LD) structure across AFR (African), EUR (European), and EAS (East Asian) populations.

Panel a: Schematic illustration of population bottlenecks. A wide arch (representing AFR) splits into two narrower arches (EUR and EAS), depicting reduced genetic diversity outside Africa.

Panel b: Density plot of minor allele frequency (MAF) at GWAS-catalog sites for three populations:
  - EAS (blue): sharply peaked near MAF = 0, indicating many low-frequency variants
  - AFR (purple): intermediate
  - EUR (red): flatter distribution with more variants at higher MAF

Panels c, d, e: Pairwise LD heatmaps (blue color scale, 0.0 to 1.0) comparing:
  - c: AFR (rows) vs EAS (columns)
  - d: EAS (rows) vs EUR (columns)
  - e: AFR (rows) vs EUR (columns)

The heatmaps show that LD block structure differs substantially across ancestries, which has implications for portability of GWAS findings and PRS across populations.
