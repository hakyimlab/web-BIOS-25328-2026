Slide titled "Performance decays with genetic distance to training population" (slide 41).

Four panels from Ding et al. 2023 Nature, showing that PGS accuracy decreases as individuals are more genetically distant from the training population.

Panel a: PCA plot of individuals colored by ancestry group (African, East Asian, European, Hispanic Latino American, South Asian, Unclassified). An arrow indicates the PGS training population cluster (European).

Panel b: Box plots of population-level PGS accuracy (y-axis) across ancestry groups (x-axis: European, Hispanic Latino American, South Asian, East Asian, African, Unclassified). Accuracy decreases progressively from European to African.

Panel c: Schematic in PCA space showing vectors d1 < d2 < d3 < d4 representing increasing genetic distances from the training population, with corresponding decrease in accuracy r1² > r2² > r3² > r4².

Panel d: Scatter plot of individual-level PGS accuracy (y-axis) vs genetic distance to training population on the genetic ancestry continuum (x-axis), colored by ancestry. Shows a clear negative correlation across individuals.

Source: Polygenic scoring accuracy varies across the genetic ancestry continuum. Ding et al 2023 Nature. https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-023-06079-4
